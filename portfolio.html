Portfolio use case

Moderated usability study for disruption operations

Executed a moderated usability study that simulated a real “day of ops” scenario for aviation operations managers. Participants were asked to manage disruptions using a prototype application while product and UX teams observed, asked questions, and collected actionable feedback.

  • Validated operational flows and acceptance criteria with real users
  • Captured usability issues, exception handling, and workflow recommendations
  • Supported iterative product decisions with moderated study results and real-time notes

This case illustrates how research-driven product design turns enterprise complexity into usable workflows, especially for high-stakes aviation disruption management.

Portfolio use case

Enterprise workscope for customer technical program teams

Designed the enterprise workscope experience for GE Aviation’s customer technical program community, enabling engineers and service planners to review engine history, approval status, and maintenance scope in a single workflow.

  • Streamlined workscope discovery with contextual engine details and approval states
  • Aligned technical program inputs to support customer communication and decision-making
  • Integrated service bulletin insights, health analytics, and version control into one interface

This example highlights how enterprise design brings a complex, multi-source aviation workflow into a coherent and actionable product experience.

Portfolio use case

Flight disruption management with solver-assisted workflows

Built a flight disruption management experience that combines schedule visualization, automated solver recommendations, and operator triage workflows to improve decision confidence.

  • Presented disruption scenarios in list and Gantt-style views for flexible triage
  • Surface solver-generated solutions while preserving user trust and control
  • Enabled prioritization, recovery tracking, and operational handoff in one interface

This use case demonstrates how UX design can make complex optimization output understandable and actionable for operations teams.
